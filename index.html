About BSED Program

The Bachelor of Secondary Education (BSED) is a four-year undergraduate program designed to prepare students for teaching in secondary schools. This program equips future educators with the necessary knowledge, skills, and values to effectively teach in their chosen area of specialization.

BSED graduates are trained to develop lesson plans, facilitate learning, assess student performance, and create a conducive learning environment. The program combines theoretical foundations with practical teaching experiences through field studies and practice teaching.

Program Events

BSED Education program implemented

An educational tour is a planned excursion undertaken by students and educators to explore educational destinations outside the classroom, aiming to enhance learning through real-world experiences.

These tours are designed to complement classroom instruction by providing hands-on activities, exposure to diverse environments and cultures, and opportunities for critical thinking.

A Search Event is a formal process or program organized by a school, institution, or organization to identify, recognize, and award individuals who have shown excellence, leadership, or outstanding performance in a specific field. These events often involve nominations, screening, interviews, and final selection of awardees or honorees.

A pre-service teacher is a student who is currently undergoing formal education and training to become a professional teacher. This includes both theoretical coursework and practical teaching experiences such as internships or student teaching. Pre-service teachers are not yet licensed or certified, but they are preparing for full-time teaching roles by developing skills in lesson planning, classroom management, and student assessment.

A Ring Hop ceremony is a tradition in higher education, particularly in the Philippines, where graduating students receive their class rings, often from parents, loved ones, or faculty. This ceremony marks a significant milestone, signifying the completion of their academic journey and their transition into the next phase of their lives, whether it's professional careers or further studies. The ring itself is a symbolic representation of the graduate's achievement and their connection to their institution.

International Education Day, observed annually on January 24th, celebrates the crucial role of education in fostering peace, sustainable development, and global unity. Declared by the United Nations General Assembly, this day recognizes education's power to transform lives, create opportunities, and build resilient societies.
